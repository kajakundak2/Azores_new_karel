What awaits you in São Miguel?
The largest of the nine Azorean islands, nicknamed the "Green Island," offers an incredible concentration of natural beauty across its 747 km². Highlights include:
🌋 Active volcanic activity and hot springs
🌊 Lakes in volcanic craters
🍵 The only tea plantation in Europe
🐋 Coastlines with whale watching opportunities
🏛️ Historic towns with a Portuguese vibe

Day 1: First Steps in Ponta Delgada
Transport from the airport and accommodation
Upon arrival, you'll have a short, three-kilometer transfer to the capital. I recommend booking a rental car at the airport in advance – you practically can't get by without one on the island.
If you want to save money, you don't need a car at all on the first day and can rent it the next morning directly in the city.
You can find the cheapest rental cars on Discovercars.com, and I’ve detailed accommodation options in this article.
Day 1 Program: Getting to know Ponta Delgada
I assume you'll be arriving in the late afternoon or evening. This short amount of time is perfect for slowly soaking up the local atmosphere; I would just wander the streets aimlessly and watch the world go by.
The next morning will be more intense. Everything is accessible on foot from the city center, so I recommend starting early.
What's on the agenda for day one
🏰 Historic Center
Start at Praça Gonçalo Velho square with its iconic triple gates. Stroll through the cobbled streets featuring characteristic Azorean architecture – a combination of white and black lava stone. Visit the São Sebastião church and the religious art museum in the former convent. You can walk through the entire center in about 2-3 hours.
🌺 Jardim António Borges
A beautiful botanical garden full of exotic plants, ponds, and mysterious hidden corners. You'll find rare species of ferns, giant bamboo, and centuries-old trees here. Admission is free, and the garden is open daily from 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM. I recommend stopping for a coffee at the café inside.
🦞 Mercado da Graça
A traditional market with fresh fish and seafood. Right next door is an appealing cheese shop called O Rei dos Queijos (The King of Cheeses).
🍍 Plantação de Ananás dos Açores
The most famous pineapple plantation on the island, with a tradition dating back to 1919. During the tour, you'll see the entire two-year process of growing pineapples in Victorian-style greenhouses. It also includes a small museum and a tasting of pineapple liqueur. Entry is free, and the tour takes about 30 minutes. Don't forget to visit the local shop, where you can buy not only fresh pineapples but also various pineapple products like jams or liqueurs.
🏊‍♂️ Praia das Milícias
A city beach with black volcanic sand, perfect for an afternoon swim. The Atlantic water is a pleasant 20-22°C (68-72°F). On the beach, you'll find showers, restrooms, and a few bars. I recommend arriving after 4:00 PM when it’s not as hot. An alternative could be the Piscinas do Pesqueiro swimming complex right in the city.
For dinner, I recommend the renowned restaurant A Tasca, where they prepare excellent seafood soup and grilled fish according to the daily catch. Prices are slightly higher – a main course will cost you €15-25, but it’s worth it.
Practical Tips:
There are paid parking lots in the center (approx. €1/hour).
For lunch, avoid the tourist trap restaurants on the main square.
Take your swimsuit and towel with you in the morning.
There is a nice café for relaxing in the botanical garden.

Day 2: The Western Part of the Island in Detail
Morning Program
🗻 Sete Cidades
Head out right after breakfast to the Vista do Rei viewpoint. From a height of 550 meters, you'll see two connected lakes – the blue one and the green one. According to legend, they were formed from the tears of a princess and a shepherd. You’ll get the best photos between 7:00 and 9:00 AM.
📸 Miradouro do Grota do Inferno
A lesser-known but all the more impressive viewpoint overlooking a volcanic crater. You drive here via a dirt road and will often have the place to yourself. Parking is a few kilometers from the viewpoint.
Afternoon Program
⛪ Igreja de São Nicolau
The first stop will be the Igreja de São Nicolau church, where you'll find free parking. From here, you have two options for a walk around Lagoa Azul – either head straight to the water and walk along the lakeshore, or choose the red-marked trail on Mapy.cz, which takes you to a higher elevation and offers beautiful views of the whole area. Both routes are undemanding and offer several picnic spots with benches.
♨️ Ponta da Ferraria
End the day in a natural thermal pool right in the ocean. The best time to visit is during low tide when the water is most pleasant (38-40°C / 100-104°F). You can find current tide times in local apps.
Practical Tips for Day Two:
Bring plenty of water for the hike (min. 2 liters per person).
Bring water shoes for Ferraria (slippery rocks).
The area might be foggy for the first half of the day.
There are only a few dining options in the area, so pack a snack.

Day 3: Northeastern Adventure
Morning Program
🌊 Santa Iria Coastal Cliffs
Majestic cliffs rising 180 meters above the ocean. Arrive at sunrise for unforgettable views. Free parking right at the Miradouro do Cintrão viewpoint.
🍵 Gorreana Tea Plantation
The only and oldest tea plantation in Europe. The tour of the tea production process is free, including a tasting. The morning hours are the most interesting, as that's when the tea harvesting takes place. You can buy tea at good prices in the local shop.
Afternoon Program
💦 Waterfalls
You'll find countless beautiful waterfalls on São Miguel island. Along the way, I recommend visiting these in particular:
Trilho Moinho do Félix – a series of three smaller waterfalls in a pleasant forest setting, where you'll also find the remains of an old mill.
Cascata da Ribeira dos Caldeirões – an easily accessible waterfall right by the road, which also includes an ethnographic museum and an old mill.
In the summer months, some of these waterfalls may dry up partially or completely, so I recommend checking their status in advance. You can drive to all these waterfalls and park nearby.
🚨 Farol do Arnel
I recommend ending the day at the iconic Farol do Arnel lighthouse, which offers impressive views of the dramatic coastal cliffs. A very steep road leads to the lighthouse – I recommend parking at the top and walking down. At sunset, the lighthouse and surrounding cliffs create a beautiful scene for photography.
(Nordeste, Farol do Arnel, Azores)
Day 4: Whales and Relaxation
Morning Program
🐋 Whale Safari
A three-hour cruise to watch whales and dolphins. The success rate for sightings is 98%. The price is around €65, including waterproof clothing rental. From personal experience, I recommend the company Futurismo. Book in advance here.
Afternoon Program
🍽️ Lunch in Ponta Delgada
The city of Ponta Delgada has plenty of great dining options. If you're craving something quick and cheap, you'll find food trucks with fast food choices a short walk from the harbor. For a healthier alternative, try the Salato salad bar. In both cases, you'll enjoy a good meal at affordable prices.
🌅 Ponta dos Mosteiros
I recommend ending the day at the impressive rock formations of Ponta dos Mosteiros, which create an unforgettable scene at sunset. The place is known for its characteristic basalt sea stacks rising from the ocean. Weather permitting, you can also take a dip in the sea here. It's the perfect spot for photography and a peaceful end to the day while watching the sun set over the Atlantic.
Day 5: The South Coast
Morning Program
🌊 Água d’Alto Beach
One of the most beautiful beaches on the island. Fine black sand and crystal-clear water. Perfect for a pleasant start to the day. Watch out for Portuguese Man o' War warnings. Free parking, showers, and a bar are available.
🌋 Vila Franca do Campo
The historic first capital of the island. Visit the local ceramics workshop and taste the famous local "queijadas" pastries.
Afternoon Program
⛪ Capela de Nossa Senhora das Vitórias
On the way to Furnas, I recommend making your first stop at the neo-Gothic chapel, Capela de Nossa Senhora das Vitórias. This impressive building is located on the shores of Lagoa das Furnas and offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape. You can walk around the chapel and soak in the peaceful atmosphere of this place.
♨️ Furnas and Caldeiras Vulcânicas
In Furnas itself, don't miss a visit to the Caldeiras Vulcânicas – active geothermal springs and fumaroles. You can taste water from various mineral springs here, each with a specific taste and healing properties.
I recommend trying the local specialty here: Cozido das Furnas – a traditional dish cooked for several hours underground using geothermal heat. It's a mix of different meats, vegetables, and sausages that gain a unique flavor from being cooked in the volcanic heat. The best Cozido is served at Terra Nostra and Tony’s restaurants.
For botany lovers, there is the Terra Nostra Park with its extensive gardens and thermal pool. Admission is €12 per person.
💦 Waterfall and Beach
You'll find the Cascata da Ribeira Quente waterfall between road tunnels. Parking is available along the road but requires caution. After seeing the waterfall, I recommend continuing down to the sea to the unique Praia do Fogo beach, where hot thermal springs mix with cold seawater.
Evening Program
♨️ Poça da Dona Beija
End your day relaxing in this complex of five thermal pools with temperatures ranging from 25 to 39°C. It's open daily from 7:00 AM to 11:00 PM (last entry at 10:30 PM), and your stay is limited to 90 minutes. Admission is €8, and I promise it’s worth it! The best atmosphere is after dark when the pools are lit up. Changing rooms with lockers are available; I recommend bringing your own towel and flip-flops.
Day 6: Flexible Program
Program Options
🗻 Lagoa do Fogo
The "Lake of Fire" at an altitude of 575m. The hike around the lake takes about 2 hours. The best views are in the morning.
🐋 Second Attempt at Whale Watching
In case you didn't see them the first time.
🎁 Souvenir Shopping in Ponta Delgada
I recommend Azorean tea (if you haven't bought it already at the Gorreana plantation), pineapple liqueur, local ceramics, and volcanic salt.
Day 7: Departure
Before Your Flight
If breakfast isn't included in your accommodation, I recommend visiting the renowned café Louvre Michaelense. Besides quality coffee, they offer a great breakfast menu and traditional Azorean desserts. The café is located in the city's historic center.
